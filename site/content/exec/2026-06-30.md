## The Week at a Glance

- 🔴 **High — Exchange Web Services (EWS) retires in October 2026.** Any application still using this legacy email integration protocol will stop working. Organizations have three months to identify and migrate affected tools.
- 🔴 **High — Active threat campaigns targeting employees via malicious browser extensions and phishing archives.** Microsoft Threat Intelligence confirmed attacks this week using fake AI-branded browser extensions and photo-themed file attachments. Both deliver persistent malware.
- 🟡 **Medium — AI agents in meetings and workflows need governance controls now.** New protections in Teams block uninvited bots, and Microsoft released new Entra and Purview guidance for governing AI identities and data access. Organizations scaling AI should confirm controls are in place before adoption outpaces policy.
- 🟢 **Low — Windows 11 point-in-time restore and Intune application management updates are generally available.** These reduce IT recovery time and endpoint vulnerability exposure with no immediate action required.

## Why This Week Matters

Three converging pressures define this week: a hard deadline on a legacy email integration that could break operational systems in October, active threat actors exploiting employee trust in AI-branded tools, and AI agents now acting autonomously inside your environment without adequate governance at most organizations. The one thing leadership must understand: AI is no longer just a productivity feature. It is software that acts, accesses data, and makes decisions on behalf of your organization. Every AI capability that ships without a parallel governance decision is a gap your auditors, insurers, and adversaries will find before you do.

## What Your Employees Will Notice

- **Teams meetings will show a prompt before any bot joins.** Microsoft has added smarter bot protection that alerts participants when an automated tool attempts to enter a meeting, particularly third-party AI note-takers. Employees may see new confirmation dialogs they have not seen before. Prepare them to pay attention to these prompts rather than dismissing them.
- **Learning Agent is now live in Microsoft 365 Copilot.** Employees with Copilot (Premium) licenses will begin receiving personalized AI skill recommendations inside Teams, Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Expect questions about what it is, whether it is monitoring their work, and how to use it.
- **Copilot in PowerPoint is coming to Government Cloud users in September.** For organizations in GCC or GCC High environments, this is the first availability of this feature. Communications should set expectations now.
- **Windows 11 devices can now be restored to a previous state in minutes.** If IT deploys point-in-time restore, employees may notice a new recovery option. This reduces, but does not eliminate, situations where a device must be fully rebuilt.
- **Some browser extension behavior may change** if IT teams respond to the AI-branded malicious extension threat by tightening browser extension policies. Employees using legitimate AI browser tools may see prompts or restrictions and should be told why in advance.

## What Your Help Desk Should Expect

- **Increased ticket volume around Teams bot prompts.** Employees will encounter new meeting bot warnings and may not know whether to allow or block the tool. Equip the help desk with a short approved/blocked list of third-party meeting bots before this generates high call volume.
- **Questions about Learning Agent in Copilot.** "Is this monitoring me?" and "How do I turn this off?" are the two most likely questions. Help desk staff should have a one-paragraph answer ready.
- **EWS-related application failures will begin appearing.** As October approaches, administrators and business users will start noticing that certain older integrations behave erratically. Help desks should be trained to recognize EWS errors and escalate to the application owners rather than spending time troubleshooting symptoms.
- **Potential credential compromise reports.** Following the StealC and Amadey takedown, employees on affected devices may have had credentials harvested. If IT initiates a credential exposure sweep, the resulting forced password resets will drive reset-related support tickets.
- **Browser restriction complaints.** If IT acts on the malicious extension advisory, employees using legitimate AI browser extensions may report that tools "stopped working." A brief internal FAQ can redirect this volume.

## Cost & Licensing

- **Microsoft 365 Copilot (Premium) license required for Learning Agent and Copilot in PowerPoint.** Organizations that have not yet purchased Copilot licenses will not have access to Learning Agent or the AI editing features rolling out this quarter. If leadership is evaluating Copilot ROI, this week's Learning Agent launch is the right moment to revisit the business case.
- **Teams Rooms Pro license required for IntelliFrame people labels**, the new feature that displays name tags for in-room participants during hybrid meetings. Organizations running Teams Rooms on Windows with lower-tier licenses will not receive this capability. Rooms used for executive or board meetings may warrant the upgrade.
- **Government Cloud Copilot parity is arriving in September.** Organizations in GCC or GCC High environments that have been deferring Copilot purchases due to feature gaps should note that Copilot in PowerPoint lands in September 2026. Budget planning for Q4 should reflect this.
- **Intune-managed application automation updates** may reduce the labor cost of manual software packaging. Organizations paying for managed service contracts where packaging is billed by effort should ask their providers how June's Intune updates affect pricing.

## If You Take No Action

**EWS retirement in October 2026:** Any application still using the legacy Exchange Web Services protocol will lose email access when Microsoft disables it. This includes integrations that may support finance workflows, HR onboarding, helpdesk ticketing, or compliance archiving. There is no grace period announced beyond October. The cost of emergency remediation at that point will far exceed the cost of an audit started now, and operational disruption to staff may be immediate and visible.

**Malicious browser extension and phishing campaign:** Both threats are live and active as of this week. Employees who install the fake AI-branded browser extension will have their search traffic silently redirected, exposing any credentials, confidential queries, or internal URLs they enter. Employees who open photo archive attachments from the hospitality-sector campaign may deliver persistent malware to your network that survives detection by traditional security tools. Either scenario can trigger cyber insurance notification obligations, regulatory breach reporting under HIPAA or state privacy laws, and reputational damage. These are not theoretical: Microsoft Threat Intelligence confirmed active campaigns this week.

**AI agent governance:** AI agents operating without defined trust boundaries will access whatever data and tools they are connected to. As your organization expands Copilot and third-party AI integrations, the absence of a governance framework today means no documented control evidence for your next SOC 2 audit, no defined incident response process if an agent exposes sensitive data, and no policy basis for employee or vendor accountability. The cost of building this framework after an incident is significantly higher than building it now.
