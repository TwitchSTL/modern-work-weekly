## The Week at a Glance

- 🔴 **High — Patch Linux systems now.** The "Dirty Frag" vulnerability is being actively exploited in the wild and allows attackers to gain full administrative control of a Linux system from a low-privileged starting point. Any Linux server or endpoint reachable via remote access, a web application, or a contractor account is at risk today.
- 🔴 **High — SAP SuccessFactors authentication change has a hard November 2026 cutoff.** If your organization uses Microsoft Entra to sync employee data into or out of SAP SuccessFactors, the current authentication method stops working in November. Missing this deadline means HR provisioning — new hires, terminations, role changes — silently breaks.
- 🟡 **Medium — AI agent identity governance is now production-ready; act within 30 days if you are deploying Copilot or custom AI agents.** Microsoft has released a formal identity and authorization framework for AI agents. Organizations deploying agents without this governance model carry growing access-control and audit risk.
- 🟢 **Low — Microsoft is signaling the end of its on-premises identity sync tool.** No deadline yet, but organizations running Entra Connect Sync on-premises should begin inventorying their dependencies and building a migration roadmap toward the cloud-native replacement.

## Why This Week Matters

The combination of an actively exploited vulnerability and two converging authentication deadlines means IT and security leaders cannot treat this as a routine update cycle. The Dirty Frag vulnerability is particularly significant because attackers only need minimal access to a Linux system — a single compromised account or web application — to achieve full control, which makes it relevant far beyond organizations that think of themselves as "Linux shops." Meanwhile, the shift away from static passwords in enterprise integrations (SAP, and more broadly across all provisioning connectors) reflects a sustained industry direction: shared credentials embedded in integrations are being eliminated, and each deadline represents a concrete operational break if missed. The one thing leadership must understand this week is that two of these items have fixed external deadlines that cannot be extended by vendor support calls — the SAP cutoff is driven by SAP's product roadmap, not Microsoft's.

## What Your Employees Will Notice

- **Mac users on new devices:** Employees receiving a new company Mac will now be signed into their corporate account immediately upon first login, with no extra setup steps required. This is an improvement — fewer support calls on day one.
- **iOS users with certificate-based authentication:** Sign-in prompts on iPhone and iPad may become less frequent for employees using certificate-based authentication, particularly in native apps. Multi-certificate users will see a cleaner, filtered certificate selection prompt.
- **Teams meeting rooms (in-person):** Meeting rooms running Teams Rooms on Windows will gain an AI-powered note-taking assistant for in-person meetings. Employees can expect auto-generated notes, decisions, and action items to appear on the room display and in meeting recaps. Notes are auto-deleted if not shared. This rollout targets July 2026.
- **Teams: shared mailbox scheduling:** Executive assistants and Chiefs of Staff will soon be able to send meeting invitations on behalf of shared or delegated mailboxes directly from Teams. This targets June 2026.
- **Teams: keyboard shortcuts search:** Power users will be able to search for keyboard shortcuts by name within Teams. Minor quality-of-life improvement; no workflow disruption.
- **Copilot Page users:** A new "Suggested Edits" feature will offer inline content improvement suggestions within Copilot Pages. Rolling out in preview during May 2026, generally available in June.
- **Android users (corporate devices, Android 14+):** Employees may notice changes to which apps handle password autofill and passkey storage, depending on policy decisions made by IT. Google Password Manager will not be available as a credential provider on corporate-owned work profile devices — employees who rely on it for personal passwords on a corporate Android device should be made aware in advance.

## What Your Help Desk Should Expect

- **Spike in Linux patch-related tickets:** Emergency kernel patching across Linux servers and endpoints will generate tickets for patch failures, service restarts, and application compatibility issues. Volume depends on Linux footprint; organizations with significant Linux infrastructure should pre-position support capacity.
- **Mac onboarding questions:** The new immediate SSO experience during Mac enrollment is an improvement, but the first cohort of users through the new flow may still contact the help desk out of habit or if pre-enrollment configuration was incomplete. Brief frontline staff on what the new experience looks like.
- **Android autofill and passkey questions:** If IT adjusts credential provider policies on Android 14+ devices, employees may find that their previously configured autofill stops working. Anticipate "my autofill broke" tickets from Android users on corporate devices.
- **SAP SuccessFactors provisioning errors (future):** No immediate ticket volume, but if migration planning does not begin, expect a wave of "new hire accounts not created" and "terminated employee still has access" tickets starting in November 2026.
- **TeamViewer remote support disruptions:** Organizations using TeamViewer for remote assistance have 12 months to migrate to the new connector. Help desk staff using the old TeamViewer connector should be informed that a migration is coming and that the old connector will eventually stop working.
- **Power BI / Intune reporting questions:** Reports built before November 2025 using the deprecated Intune Data Warehouse beta connector may produce errors. Analysts or managers relying on those dashboards should be directed to IT for connector migration.
- **Windows 11 25H2 security baseline:** IT teams should be aware that existing Windows security baseline profiles do not auto-update to the new 25H2 baseline. Until profiles are manually updated, devices may show policy gaps in compliance reporting — expect questions from security-aware managers reviewing dashboards.

## Cost & Licensing

- **Purview Data Lifecycle Management — new AI data retention charge:** Retaining generative AI prompts and responses (non-Microsoft 365 sources) under DLM policies now incurs a charge of $0.25 per GB per month. Organizations that have enabled retention policies covering Copilot or third-party AI activity should model their data volume against this rate before the next billing cycle. This is a net-new cost line for many organizations.
- **Teams Rooms Pro required for AI Facilitator Agent:** The new in-person meeting AI agent feature requires a Teams Rooms Pro license. Organizations running Teams Rooms on Windows with standard licenses will not receive this capability. Evaluate whether the productivity benefit justifies upgrading affected rooms.
- **Entra license usage visibility:** A new page in the Entra Admin Center now shows actual consumption of Entra ID P1, P2, and Suite licenses mapped to feature usage, with six months of trend data. This is a useful input for upcoming license renewal negotiations — IT should pull this report before any Microsoft licensing conversation.
- **Global Secure Access expansion:** Several new GSA capabilities (iOS client, cloud firewall for remote networks, network content filtering by file type) are now generally available. Organizations evaluating whether GSA justifies its licensing cost should revisit the business case given this expanded feature set.

## If You Take No Action

**Dirty Frag (Linux LPE):** An attacker who gains any low-level access to an unpatched Linux system — through a phishing email, a compromised contractor credential, or a vulnerable web application — can escalate to full administrative control of that system in seconds. From there, they can move laterally across your network, exfiltrate data, deploy ransomware, or establish persistent access. Microsoft Defender can detect exploitation attempts but detection is not a substitute for patching. Cyber insurance policies increasingly exclude coverage for incidents involving known, unpatched vulnerabilities, particularly those listed in CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog.

**SAP SuccessFactors basic auth deprecation:** If your organization does not migrate before SAP's November 2026 cutoff, the automated connection between Microsoft Entra and SuccessFactors will stop authenticating. The immediate visible consequence is that new hire accounts will stop being created automatically, terminated employees' accounts may not be deprovisioned on schedule, and role changes will not propagate. Beyond the operational disruption, failure to deprovision terminated employees in a timely manner is a direct compliance exposure under SOC 2, HIPAA, and GDPR, and is a common finding in access control audits.

**AI Agent Identity Governance (Entra Agent ID):** AI agents deployed without formal identity governance operate with access that is not fully auditable, may not be bound by Conditional Access policies, and cannot be cleanly reviewed or revoked. As agent deployments scale — and they will scale — the gap between what agents can access and what your organization knows about becomes a material audit and regulatory risk. The Entra Agent ID framework is now available at no additional cost for organizations with existing Entra licensing; the risk of inaction is an access governance debt that compounds with each new agent deployed.
