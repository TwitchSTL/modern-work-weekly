## The Week at a Glance

- 🔴 **High — Storm-2949 breach: credentials alone wiped a cloud environment.** This is not a future threat. Microsoft has published the full attack playbook. Your identity controls need to be reviewed this week — before an attacker uses the same method against your organization.
- 🟡 **Medium — Copilot in Word now lets users choose Anthropic AI models.** This is live now. Without updated usage policies and user communication, employees may share business data with a third-party AI provider under terms your organization has not reviewed.

## Why This Week Matters

The Storm-2949 incident is the clearest illustration yet that strong passwords alone are not an identity security strategy. The attacker used legitimate credentials, moved through trusted systems, and exfiltrated data at scale — all without triggering malware-based defenses. Every organization running Microsoft 365 operates in this same environment.

At the same time, Microsoft is expanding Copilot's AI model options and bringing new data lifecycle automation to market. Both developments are net positive — but they arrive with governance decisions your legal, compliance, and HR leadership need to make before employees encounter them organically.

The one thing leadership must understand this week: **your identity controls are your perimeter.** If Conditional Access policies, privileged access governance, and anomalous sign-in detection are not actively maintained, your organization has the same exposure profile as the Storm-2949 victim.

## What Your Employees Will Notice

- **Copilot in Word now shows a model selector.** Employees using Microsoft 365 Copilot in Word will see the option to switch from the default OpenAI model to an Anthropic model. Without proactive communication, many will experiment without understanding the data implications. Send guidance before this becomes a help desk question.
- **Meeting recaps now include AI-narrated video highlights.** Users with access to Teams Intelligent Recap will see short video clips and AI-generated takeaways from recorded meetings — not just transcripts. Those who did not know meetings were being summarized this way may be surprised. Confirm your recording and recap policies are communicated clearly.
- **Walk-up room booking arrives this summer.** Employees will be able to book a conference room directly from the Teams Room console without opening a calendar app. This is a convenience win, but it changes room reservation behavior — facilities teams should be aware.
- **AI-generated meeting notes for in-person rooms (July).** In Teams Rooms on Android, a tap on the room console will start real-time AI note-taking for in-person meetings. Notes appear on the main screen and are deleted after the meeting unless someone saves them. Employees will need to know this exists and how it works before they walk in and see it happening.
- **Teams meeting video gallery is now resizable.** A small but visible change — users can resize the participant video strip during screen sharing. No training required, but worth mentioning in any Teams tips communication.

## What Your Help Desk Should Expect

- **"Why does Copilot in Word show different AI options?"** — Ticket volume expected as users discover the Anthropic model selector and ask what it means, whether they can use it, and whether their work is safe. Prepare a one-paragraph FAQ for frontline staff.
- **"My meeting recap looks different — there are video clips now."** — Video recap is already rolling. Staff unfamiliar with Intelligent Recap will file tickets asking what it is and whether they can turn it off. Ensure your help desk knows the feature and your organization's recording policy.
- **Teams Rooms auto-remediation may reduce a category of tickets.** Audio, video, and display issues on Pro-licensed rooms should self-resolve overnight in many cases. If your team tracks room-related tickets, watch for a volume drop as a signal that auto-remediation is working — or an absence of that drop as a signal that Pro licensing is not in place on all rooms.
- **Identity and access review activity may surface questions.** If your IT team conducts the Storm-2949-prompted identity audit recommended this week, employees whose access is adjusted or whose MFA requirements change will contact the help desk. Brief your team in advance so they can answer confidently.
- **Walk-up room booking questions (starting July).** "How do I book a room from the screen?" will become a new ticket category once ad-hoc reservation goes GA. Prepare short in-room signage or a help article ahead of rollout.

## Cost & Licensing

- **Teams Rooms Pro is the activation gate for multiple new capabilities.** Auto-remediation, AI meeting notes (Facilitator Agent), ad-hoc room booking, and digital signage on Android all require the Teams Rooms Pro license. Organizations running Teams Rooms on the standard license tier will not receive these features. If you have unmanaged or mixed-license room deployments, now is the time to audit and consolidate — the operational savings from auto-remediation alone may justify the upgrade cost.
- **Purview lifecycle archiving will reduce OneDrive and SharePoint storage costs.** Inactive files will automatically move to lower-cost storage tiers while remaining fully compliant and searchable. Organizations with large SharePoint and OneDrive footprints should begin sizing this opportunity now — the storage cost reduction could be material depending on your data volume. This feature requires appropriate Purview licensing; confirm your subscription covers Data Lifecycle Management.
- **Copilot model selection carries no additional licensing cost at this time**, but using Anthropic models through Microsoft's Copilot platform is subject to Microsoft's data handling commitments — not a direct Anthropic relationship. Your legal team should confirm this distinction before communicating it to employees.

## If You Take No Action

**On Storm-2949 identity controls:** The attack method Microsoft documented this week — compromised credentials, no malware, full cloud exfiltration — is reproducible against any organization whose identity governance has not been actively maintained. If Conditional Access is not enforcing strong authentication consistently, if privileged access is not scoped and time-limited, and if anomalous sign-in behavior is not generating alerts, your organization has no reliable mechanism to detect or stop this class of attack. A breach of this type carries regulatory notification obligations, potential legal liability, and reputational consequences that far exceed the cost of the controls that prevent it.

**On Copilot Anthropic model selection:** Without a usage policy and employee communication, business data — including potentially sensitive or regulated content — will flow through a model selection that employees do not fully understand and that your compliance and legal teams have not reviewed. The organizational risk is not that Microsoft's platform is unsafe; it is that employees will make data-sharing decisions without context, and your organization will have no defensible record of having addressed the question.

**On Purview lifecycle archiving (July GA):** Organizations that arrive at July without retention policies scoped will not automatically benefit from the cost reduction or compliance coverage this feature provides. Stale and sensitive data will continue accumulating in OneDrive and SharePoint, carrying ongoing storage cost and regulatory exposure. The window to scope and deploy policy before GA is available now — inaction simply delays a return that could otherwise arrive on day one.
