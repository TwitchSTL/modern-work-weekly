## The Week at a Glance

- 🔴 **High — Exchange Server security patch required now.** Microsoft released June 2026 security updates for Exchange Server SE, 2019, and 2016. A separate OWA vulnerability (CVE-2026-42897) disclosed in May allows arbitrary JavaScript execution when a user opens a crafted email. Organizations running on-premises Exchange must apply patches immediately or accept active exploit risk.
- 🔴 **High — Exchange Web Services (EWS) retirement enters its final phase, deadline October 2026.** Microsoft has introduced an allow-list tool to help IT teams identify which applications still depend on the legacy EWS API before it is disabled. Organizations with custom applications, third-party tools, or hybrid mail flow that have not yet migrated face a hard cutoff in roughly four months.
- 🟡 **Medium — Advanced Intune capabilities arrive in M365 E3 and E5 on July 1; tenant delivery by August 1.** Organizations that have not reviewed what these expanded device management capabilities unlock should plan now, both to capture security value and to prepare help desks and end users for new policy enforcement options.
- 🟢 **Low — Microsoft 365 Copilot adds multiple new AI models, agents, and a redesigned interface.** Copilot Cowork is now generally available, new AI models from Anthropic and OpenAI are rolling out, and Microsoft Scout launches as an always-on personal agent. These expand what licensed users can do but require no immediate IT action.

## Why This Week Matters

Two independent deadlines are converging in a way that demands leadership attention. On-premises Exchange servers carry an actively disclosed vulnerability while a four-month clock ticks toward the permanent retirement of a legacy email API that thousands of business applications still rely on. Organizations that treat either item as a routine IT task without allocating time, budget, or vendor coordination risk a compliance gap, a service outage, or both before Q4 begins.

At the same time, the volume and speed of AI agent capabilities now reaching general availability means that governance decisions deferred during the pilot phase are becoming urgent. Agents are no longer experimental; they are production features operating on real business data, and the controls that govern them need to be in place before adoption outpaces oversight.

## What Your Employees Will Notice

- **Copilot looks and behaves differently.** The Microsoft 365 Copilot app has been redesigned with a cleaner interface. A new toggle makes it easy to switch between everyday chat and the deeper, multi-step Copilot Cowork experience. Users with Copilot licenses may notice the change without being warned.
- **Copilot gives richer answers using images.** Copilot can now interpret charts, diagrams, and screenshots embedded in Word documents, PowerPoint files, and PDFs to provide more complete responses. Users may find answers more detailed than before.
- **Voice enrollment in Teams is easier.** Employees will see a prompt to register their voice profile simply by speaking during a meeting, enabling speaker recognition and more accurate meeting transcripts and recaps.
- **Teams scheduling staff will see auto-assignment of open shifts.** Frontline managers using Teams Shifts will gain the ability to automatically assign open shifts based on availability, time-off, and scheduling rules, reducing manual scheduling effort.
- **Wi-Fi-based workplace check-in is available in Teams and Microsoft Places.** Employees who opt in can have their workplace location updated automatically when they connect to a configured office network, removing the need to manually update presence.
- **Microsoft Scout is now available.** This new always-on personal agent is integrated across Microsoft 365 apps. Users may begin seeing it without prior communication from IT.
- **New AI models are available inside Copilot.** Anthropic Claude Fable 5 (preview) and OpenAI GPT-5.5 Instant are now accessible within Copilot Chat and Copilot Studio. Users with appropriate licenses will see expanded model options.
- **Mac users of Office need to update.** Security patches for CVE-2026-45469 (Excel) and CVE-2026-45475 (Office) are available for Mac. Employees running older versions remain vulnerable to remote code execution.

## What Your Help Desk Should Expect

- **Exchange patch inquiries and escalations.** If your organization runs on-premises Exchange, expect questions about patch scheduling, OWA availability, and the scope of CVE-2026-42897. Prepare a brief statement on your patching timeline.
- **Copilot interface confusion.** The redesigned Copilot app and the new Cowork toggle will generate tickets from users unfamiliar with the new layout. Proactive communication reduces volume significantly.
- **Teams voice enrollment prompts.** Users will see in-meeting prompts to enroll their voice profile. Expect questions about what data is collected and whether participation is mandatory.
- **Microsoft Scout discovery questions.** Users encountering Scout for the first time may contact IT to ask what it is, whether it is approved, and what data it accesses.
- **Mac Office update requests.** Security-conscious users or managed device policies requiring patching will drive Mac update tickets following the Excel and Office CVE disclosures.
- **Mobile email access disruptions.** If your organization uses certificate-based authentication for mobile email (Exchange ActiveSync with CBA), affected users will lose access as the retirement rolls out. Help desk should be briefed on the workaround: migration to Entra ID-based authentication.
- **EWS application failures.** Third-party or custom applications that use the Exchange Web Services API may begin behaving erratically as Microsoft enforces restrictions ahead of the October deadline. Help desk should escalate these immediately to IT application owners rather than attempting first-line resolution.
- **Intune policy changes after August 1.** As advanced Intune capabilities land in E3 and E5 tenants, users may encounter new compliance prompts or device restrictions. Help desk should be briefed before the capability rollout date.

## Cost & Licensing

- **Advanced Intune Suite capabilities are included in M365 E3 and E5 starting July 1.** Organizations paying for standalone Intune Suite add-ons should review whether those purchases remain necessary after the packaging change. This is a potential cost-reduction opportunity.
- **New Microsoft 365 Business with Copilot SKUs launch July 1.** Microsoft is introducing small-business SKUs with Copilot built in. Organizations with small-business subsidiaries or affiliates on legacy plans should evaluate whether migration to the new SKUs consolidates licensing costs.
- **Purview Data Lifecycle Management billing model is changing.** Retention of non-Microsoft 365 generative AI prompts and responses will move to a volume-based billing model, charged at approximately $0.25 per GB per month. Organizations retaining large volumes of AI interaction data should model the cost impact before the change takes effect.
- **Exchange 2016 and 2019 Extended Security Update (ESU) Period 2 is now available.** Organizations that did not complete migration by April 2026 can enroll in the Period 2 ESU program running through approximately October 2026. This is a paid program and represents an ongoing cost for organizations that have not yet migrated to Exchange Server Subscription Edition.
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot ISO 42001 recertification.** For organizations using Copilot compliance posture to satisfy vendor assessments or cyber insurance questionnaires, Copilot has achieved its second consecutive ISO/IEC 42001 recertification with zero non-conformities. This may reduce compliance documentation burden.

## If You Take No Action

**On the Exchange Server security patch:** The CVE-2026-42897 vulnerability requires only that a user open a crafted email in Outlook Web Access for JavaScript to execute in their browser session. This is a low-friction attack vector. An unpatched server is an active liability. Organizations in regulated industries, or those carrying cyber insurance with patch-currency requirements, face both breach risk and potential coverage disputes if an incident occurs on a known, unpatched system.

**On the EWS retirement deadline:** When Microsoft disables Exchange Web Services in October 2026, any application, integration, or workflow that has not been migrated to Microsoft Graph will stop working. The most common failures will be third-party archiving tools, line-of-business applications that read or send email, calendar synchronization with external systems, and hybrid mail flow configurations. Service disruptions of this type typically take weeks to remediate if vendor involvement is required, well beyond the October deadline.

**On Entra ID authentication changes:** Organizations that have not migrated away from Custom MFA controls or updated their Conditional Access configurations will experience authentication failures for users enrolled in those flows. Self-service password reset may also break for users whose methods are not explicitly registered under the new requirements. Help desk volume spikes, potential lockouts, and failed access to business-critical applications are the likely operational consequences.
