## Top 5 This Week

1. **Passkeys are now the default Entra ID authentication method.** Microsoft flipped the switch: passkeys replace passwords as the default sign-in experience in Entra ID, and SMS/voice authentication moves to a new model. If you haven't mapped your auth policy and user readiness yet, this week is the forcing function.

2. **GPT-5.6 is live in Microsoft 365 Copilot today.** OpenAI's GPT-5.6 family is now the preferred model in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Chat, and Cowork. Stronger multi-step reasoning means agentic workflows behave differently — validate your Copilot governance and prompt policies against the new model.

3. **ShinyHunters OAuth abuse targeting SaaS apps.** Microsoft Threat Intelligence published a detailed breakdown of vishing, supply-chain compromise, and misconfigured guest access being weaponized against SaaS OAuth flows. Review your OAuth app consent policies and guest access configurations now.

4. **Cross-Tenant Message Recall hits GA in Exchange Online.** Recall now works across organization boundaries, not just within a single tenant. This is one of the most-requested recall enhancements and has immediate operational implications for multi-tenant and partner-connected environments.

5. **RDP Multipath with redundant TCP is GA for Windows 365 and AVD.** Session resilience over TCP-constrained networks is now production-ready. Organizations running Cloud PCs over VPN or proxy-restricted paths should plan rollout to reduce session drops.

## Apps

- **[GPT-5.6 available today in Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-365-copilot-blog/available-today-openai-s-gpt-5-6-in-microsoft-365-copilot/ba-p/4533152)** [GA] — GPT-5.6 is now the preferred model in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Copilot Chat, and Cowork, replacing the previous default. The model delivers stronger reasoning for multi-step agentic tasks, which means outputs and behaviors may differ from what users expect. Review your Copilot usage policies and test any automated or agent-driven workflows against the new model before broad user communications.

- **[Cross-Tenant Message Recall in Exchange Online](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/exchange-team-blog/cross-tenant-message-recall-in-exchange-online/ba-p/4535800)** [GA] — Message Recall now works across tenant boundaries, closing the gap that previously limited recall to single-tenant scenarios. Organizations operating in multi-tenant or federated-partner configurations should update runbooks and communicate the capability to legal, compliance, and helpdesk teams. No admin action required to enable, but awareness and policy alignment are warranted.

- **[SharePoint Showcase: Copilot in SharePoint real-world usage and new GA features](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-sharepoint-blog/sharepoint-showcase-how-microsoft-uses-copilot-in-sharepoint-and/ba-p/4533156)** [GA] — Microsoft published its internal use cases for Copilot in SharePoint alongside a monthly "Product Drops" recap of newly GA SharePoint features. - **[Protecting Microsoft at AI speed: How SFI proactively hardens cloud services](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/07/08/protecting-microsoft-at-ai-speed-how-sfi-proactively-hardens-our-cloud/)** [GA] — Microsoft details how the Secure Future Initiative uses AI-driven continuous evaluation to measure live services against security requirements at scale. Relevant context for engineers building AI-assisted security operations or justifying SFI-aligned controls to leadership.

- **[CVE-2026-59874 node-tar: Negative tar entry size causes infinite loop](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-59874)** [GA] — A malformed tar archive with a negative entry size can cause an infinite loop in node-tar. If your M365 extensibility, SPFx, or DevOps pipelines use node-tar in any build or deploy step, verify you are on a patched version. Impact scope depends on whether the component is exposed to untrusted archive input.
