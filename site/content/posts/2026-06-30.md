## Top 5 This Week

1. **EWS retirement enters final phase, October 2026 deadline confirmed.** Microsoft has introduced `EWSAllowedAppIDs` as a controlled allow-list mechanism ahead of phased EWS disablement in Exchange Online. If you still have workloads on EWS that have not migrated to Microsoft Graph, this is your last on-ramp before the switch is thrown.

2. **Six new CVEs published across libxml2, GNU gzip, Nmap, ACL, and AGP/AMD64.** The mix of buffer overflows, symlink traversal privilege escalations, and an integer underflow spans both Linux subsystem components surfaced in Microsoft's MSRC and Windows-adjacent tooling. Review exposure across your endpoint fleet, WSL environments, and any Linux-based workloads.

3. **Teams gets automated bot protection for meetings.** Microsoft is shipping detection and controls to block uninvited bots from joining Teams meetings, directly addressing the scenario where third-party AI note-takers re-join future meetings without explicit approval. Review your tenant's meeting policies and third-party app permissions now.

4. **June Intune release ships with AI-agent-readiness framing.** The June Intune update centers compliance, patch currency, and secure enrollment as the three fundamentals required for trustworthy AI agent operation on endpoints. Application currency improvements and reduced vulnerability exposure are the headline capabilities.

5. **Malicious Chromium extension spoofing Perplexity AI confirmed hijacking browser search.** The extension uses MV3 APIs and intermediary infrastructure to redirect search traffic. If your organization allows unmanaged browser extension installs, this is a concrete example of why that policy needs review.

## Visibility & Automation

- **[Chromium Extension Uses AI-Related Branding to Redirect Browser Search](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/06/29/chromium-extension-uses-airelated-branding-redirect-browser-search/)** [GA] — A malicious extension spoofing Perplexity AI uses MV3 APIs and intermediary infrastructure to silently redirect search traffic. If your Defender for Endpoint or Intune policies do not control which browser extensions users can install, this is a concrete threat to act on. Review your browser extension allow/block lists and consider pushing a managed extension policy via Intune.

- **[CVE-2026-11979: Stack-Based Buffer Overflow in libxml2](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-11979)** [GA] — Stack-based buffer overflow in libxml2 published by MSRC. Assess exposure across any Windows or Linux workloads parsing XML via libxml2, including WSL environments and containerized apps. Apply available patches when released.

- **[CVE-2026-41992 / CVE-2026-41991: Global Buffer Overflow and Predictable Temporary File in GNU gzip](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-41992)** [GA] — Two gzip vulnerabilities published together: a global buffer overflow and a predictable temp file issue that could be exploited for privilege escalation. Both affect GNU gzip and are relevant to Linux-based workloads and WSL. Patch promptly and validate gzip versions across your Linux fleet.

- **[CVE-2026-54371 / CVE-2026-54369: Symlink Traversal Privilege Escalation via attr and acl Libraries](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-54371)** [GA] — Two symlink traversal privilege escalation CVEs affecting `attr < 2.6.0` and `acl < 2.4.0`. These are Linux filesystem attribute and access control libraries present in most distributions. Verify versions on any Linux endpoints, servers, and WSL instances and update to patched versions.

- **[CVE-2026-53325: Broken Error Propagation in agp/amd64](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-53325)** [GA] — Kernel-level issue in the AGP/AMD64 driver affecting error propagation. Scope is narrower than the other CVEs this week but relevant for environments running older AMD64 systems with AGP. Review applicability and patch as part of your regular kernel update cycle.

- **[CVE-2026-58058: Integer Underflow in Nmap IPv6 Extension Header Parsing](https://msrc.microsoft.com/update-guide/vulnerability/CVE-2026-58058)** [GA] — Integer underflow in Nmap's IPv6 extension header parsing. Relevant if your team uses Nmap for network scanning from managed endpoints or in automation pipelines. Update Nmap to a patched version and validate any scan scripts that process IPv6 targets.
