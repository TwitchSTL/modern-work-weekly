## Top 5 This Week

1. **Entra ID retiring Custom Controls, enforcing CA on credential registration, and requiring explicit SSPR method registration** — Three legacy auth gaps are closing simultaneously. If your MFA or SSPR configurations rely on Custom Controls or unregistered methods, you have an active migration obligation, not a future one. Read the [security updates post](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/microsoft-entra-id-security-updates-what-organizations-need-to/ba-p/4522024) and start your migration plan now.

2. **Computer-using agents in Copilot Studio hit GA** — Agents can now interact with any UI-driven application, vendor portal, or legacy line-of-business system without RPA scripts or exposed APIs. This is a significant automation surface expansion; your governance and data-handling policies for agents need to cover UI-driven workflows immediately.

3. **Purview DSPM for AI is GA, with cross-cloud coverage extending to AWS Bedrock** — You can now apply a single Purview policy engine across Microsoft, Azure, and AWS Bedrock AI workloads. If your org is running AI agents outside Azure, this is the governance story to evaluate this quarter.

4. **Purview Data Security Posture Reports are GA, with custom workspaces in preview** — Out-of-the-box reports now give you an outcome-based view of sensitivity label and DLP coverage across M365. This is your audit-readiness and executive reporting tool; if you haven't looked at Posture Reports yet, now is the time.

5. **Exchange 2016/2019 ESU Period 2 announced (May 2026 through April 2027)** — If you're still on Exchange 2016 or 2019 and missed the Period 1 ESU window, there's a second program available. Enrollment is required; this doesn't happen automatically, and running unpatched on end-of-support Exchange is not acceptable.

## Identity

- **[Microsoft Entra ID security updates: What organizations need to do now](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/microsoft-entra-id-security-updates-what-organizations-need-to/ba-p/4522024)** [GA] — Three breaking changes are converging: Custom Controls retirement (migrate to External MFA providers), Conditional Access enforcement during credential registration, and a requirement that SSPR users have explicitly registered authentication methods. Existing configurations will keep working during a transition window, but you need a migration plan in hand now. Audit your CA policies and SSPR registrations this week and identify any dependencies on Custom Controls.

- **[Find shadow tenants and reduce risk fast with Microsoft Entra Tenant Governance](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/find-shadow-tenants-and-reduce-risk-fast-with-microsoft-entra/ba-p/4521996)** [GA] — Entra Tenant Governance can now surface related tenants connected via B2B collaboration, multitenant apps, and shared billing, giving central IT visibility into acquisition, dev, and partner tenants that have drifted outside governance. Run tenant discovery against your environment and use the results to build a remediation backlog for any orphaned or unmanaged tenants.

- **[AI is accelerating cyberattacks — here's how to stay ahead](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/ai-is-accelerating-cyberattacks-here-s-how-to-stay-ahead/ba-p/4528592)** [GA] — Microsoft's position is that AI is enabling attackers to automate reconnaissance, credential analysis, and social engineering at scale, making identity the highest-value target. The practical takeaway: phishing-resistant MFA, Conditional Access for all privileged paths, and continuous access evaluation are non-negotiable table stakes. Review your privileged account coverage in Entra ID Protection and close any gaps.

- **[Secure external attachments with Purview encryption](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-purview-blog/secure-external-attachments-with-purview-encryption/ba-p/4463114)** [GA] — Purview-encrypted attachments sent externally behave very differently depending on whether guest accounts are provisioned: with guests enabled, access is smooth but you can generate thousands of guest accounts rapidly; without guests, external recipients hit access walls. Decide your guest account policy before rolling out Purview email encryption broadly, and consider the administrative overhead of guest account sprawl at scale.

- **[Reducing NTLM Dependency: IAKerb and LocalKDC in Windows Insider Preview](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/windows-it-pro-blog/reducing-ntlm-dependency-iakerb-and-localkdc-in-windows-insider/ba-p/4524615)** [Preview] — IAKerb and LocalKDC extend Kerberos to scenarios that previously required NTLM fallback, coming to the Canary Channel this month. For orgs actively working to block NTLM, this is the technology path forward, though it's preview-only for now. Watch for the broader rollout and factor it into your NTLM blocking roadmap.
