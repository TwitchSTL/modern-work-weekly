## Top 5 This Week

1. **Entra ID retiring Custom Controls, enforcing CA during credential registration, and requiring explicit SSPR method registration.** Existing configs continue working during transition, but migration planning must start now. Read the details in the Identity section.

2. **Computer-using agents in Copilot Studio are now GA.** Agents can now drive UI-based workflows in vendor portals and legacy line-of-business systems without RPA scripts or API dependencies. Governance and oversight policies should be in place before broad rollout.

3. **Copilot Studio gets a rebuilt authoring experience (Preview).** The new AI core and streamlined canvas target multi-step, multi-agent workflows. Evaluate for your internal makers now while it's in preview, before it becomes the default surface.

4. **Purview DSPM for AI is GA with cross-cloud coverage.** It now extends to AWS Bedrock agents and Azure AI Foundry workloads, giving you a single governance pane across cloud AI deployments. If you have AI workloads outside Azure, this changes your posture reporting story.

5. **Exchange 2016/2019 ESU Period 2 announced (May–October 2026).** If you're still on Exchange 2016 or 2019, you now have a Period 2 ESU window, but this is borrowed time. Migration to Exchange SE should be the active priority, not the fallback plan.

## Identity

- **[Microsoft Entra ID security updates: What organizations need to do now](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/microsoft-entra-id-security-updates-what-organizations-need-to/ba-p/4522024)** [GA] — Three legacy capabilities are on the retirement path: Custom Controls are being replaced by External MFA, Conditional Access will be enforced consistently during credential registration, and SSPR will require explicitly registered authentication methods. Existing configurations keep working during transition, but the window to migrate without disruption is finite. Start by auditing any Custom Controls integrations and verifying your CA policies cover credential registration flows.

- **[AI is accelerating cyberattacks — here's how to stay ahead](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/ai-is-accelerating-cyberattacks-here-s-how-to-stay-ahead/ba-p/4528592)** [GA] — Microsoft details how AI is compressing the attack chain, specifically around credential analysis, privileged account targeting, and adaptive social engineering at scale. Identity remains the primary entry point. Use this as a prompt to review your Entra ID Protection risk policies, MFA coverage gaps, and PIM activation controls.

## Action Required

- **[Microsoft Entra ID security updates: Custom Controls, CA enforcement, and SSPR changes](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-entra-blog/microsoft-entra-id-security-updates-what-organizations-need-to/ba-p/4522024)** [GA] — **Begin migration planning now.** Three retirement tracks are active: Custom Controls to External MFA, CA enforcement during credential registration, and explicit method registration for SSPR. Existing configs work during transition but the retirement timeline is firm. Audit Custom Controls integrations this week, review CA policy scope, and verify SSPR method registration completeness before the retirement dates force the change.

- **[Exchange 2016/2019 ESU Period 2: May–October 2026 deadline](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/exchange-team-blog/announcing-period-2-exchange-2016-2019-extended-security-update/ba-p/4511603)** [GA] — **October 2026 is the hard stop.** Period 2 ESU requires active enrollment and covers May through October 2026 only. Any Exchange 2016 or 2019 servers still on-premises after October 2026 will be unpatched. If you have not enrolled in Period 2 ESU, do so immediately. Simultaneously, your Exchange SE migration must be the active work stream, not a backlog item.

- **[Purview Endpoint DLP — Policy Sync & Device Health AI-Powered Investigation (Preview July 2026)](https://www.microsoft.com/microsoft-365/roadmap?id=562016)** [Preview] — **Preview arrives July 2026, GA August 2026.** Before this tooling lands, establish a baseline of your current Endpoint DLP policy sync health and device coverage. Admins who don't have a clear picture of current sync failures will struggle to distinguish pre-existing issues from new signals when the AI investigation features go live. Run a DLP device health audit now.

- **[Securing local AI agents on endpoints](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/t5/microsoft-security-community/securing-the-new-risk-surface-local-agents-claws-and-open/ba-p/4524602)** [GA] — **Governance gap requiring immediate attention.** Local AI agents running persistently on endpoints operate with deep system access and currently have minimal standardized oversight. Before your organization sees broader AI agent deployment on managed devices, define your policy: which agents are permitted, what data they can access, how their process behavior is monitored, and who owns the approval workflow. Defender for Endpoint behavioral detection rules should be reviewed for coverage of persistent agent processes.
