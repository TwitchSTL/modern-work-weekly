Modern Work Weekly is a weekly digest built specifically for **Modern Work Engineers** — the people responsible for designing, deploying, and maintaining Microsoft 365 environments. Not IT generalists. Not executives. Engineers.

---

## What this is

Every week, Microsoft publishes changes across a sprawling set of portals — Intune, Entra, Defender, Purview, the M365 Roadmap, and more. Most of it goes unread. Some of it has deadlines. A few items will quietly break something in your environment if you miss them.

Modern Work Weekly collects, filters, and summarizes the changes that actually matter to engineers running modern managed environments. No marketing, no fluff — operational signal only.

---

## What's covered

- **Endpoint management** — Intune for Windows, macOS, iOS/Android. Configuration policies, compliance, enrollment, Autopatch, and the Graph/PowerShell automation hooks underneath.
- **Identity & access** — Entra ID, Conditional Access, External MFA, hybrid sync (Connect Sync → Cloud Sync), and the evolving agent identity model.
- **Defender for Endpoint & MDO** — XDR updates, attack disruption, identity-based detections, and Defender for Office 365 changes that affect your mail flow and threat posture.
- **Purview** — Sensitivity labels, DLP policy changes, Information Protection, Insider Risk, and AI data governance as Microsoft expands Purview's scope into the AI era.
- **Licensing** — E3/E5 differences, Business Premium, Business Premium + Defender/Purview Suite Add-On, and Copilot licensing decisions — including the guardrails you should have in place before users go live.
- **Zero Trust** — Not a framework slide. Practical changes to Conditional Access, device compliance, network access (Global Secure Access), and identity posture that move the needle.
- **AI & Copilot governance** — Agent 365, Shadow AI controls, Copilot guardrails, and the fast-moving intersection of AI agents and enterprise security policy.

---

## What to expect right now

Modern Work Weekly is in **early testing**. The scraper, the formatting, and the publish pipeline are being validated. Posts may be irregular in the early weeks as the workflow matures.

What won't change: the scope, the audience, and the standard. If it's not useful to a working Modern Work Engineer, it won't be here.
