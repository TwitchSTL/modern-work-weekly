TITLE: Modern Work Weekly — Week of 2026-06-16

Microsoft Agent 365 is now generally available, and the governance question your leadership team has been avoiding just became urgent.

**TOP 5 THIS WEEK**

1. **Microsoft Agent 365 GA:** The control plane for observing, governing, and securing AI agents across your environment is out of preview. If agent sprawl is already a concern in your org, this is the tool to evaluate now — not next quarter.

2. **Exchange ActiveSync CBA deprecation:** Direct certificate-based authentication to Exchange Online via EAS will be retired by end of 2026. Teams running hybrid or legacy mobile configurations should inventory affected devices and plan migration paths before the deadline.

3. **Intune support for Android XR:** Microsoft Intune now supports Android XR platform management, including the Samsung Galaxy XR headset. Admins at orgs piloting extended reality hardware can begin building enrollment and compliance policies today.

4. **Exchange Server June 2026 Security Updates released:** Updates are available for Exchange SE, 2019, and 2016. Patch now — CVE-2026-42897 was already the subject of an out-of-band advisory earlier this month, and June's SU addresses it formally.

5. **Platform SSO for macOS automated enrollment is GA:** Combining streamlined DEP enrollment with strong identity is now fully supported. If your org has been waiting on GA status before rolling this out to your Mac fleet, the hold is lifted.

**WORTH YOUR ATTENTION**

- Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat have achieved ISO/IEC 42001:2023 recertification for a second consecutive year — useful evidence when your compliance or legal teams field AI governance questions from auditors or regulators.

- Windows 365 for Agents is now in public preview, offering isolated, cloud-hosted execution environments for AI agents at enterprise scale — a meaningful infrastructure shift that security architects should review before adoption accelerates.

- Microsoft Purview Data Security Posture Reports are now in public preview, offering outcome-based views of whether your data protection controls are actually working — not just deployed.

**ONE FOR THE HELP DESK**

Teams now honors the Windows Do Not Disturb setting by pausing notifications automatically. Users who previously relied on Teams notifications while DND was active for other reasons will notice the change without having done anything themselves — expect tickets framed as "Teams notifications stopped working."
