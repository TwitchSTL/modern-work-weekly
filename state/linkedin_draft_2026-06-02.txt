Secure Boot certificates are starting to expire in June, and if your org hasn't checked readiness, that window is closing fast.

TOP 5 THIS WEEK 🗓️

- Secure Boot certificate expiry is live: the first certs expire this month, an AMA runs June 4, and the updated Autopatch report now shows device-level certificate status and trust configuration — check it before your help desk does
- Teams live events retire June 30: the associated Graph APIs go with them, so any automation or integrations built on live events need to be migrated to Town Halls before end of month
- Exchange ActiveSync certificate-based authentication is being deprecated by end of 2026: direct CBA to Exchange Online will stop working, and any device or app relying on it needs a remediation plan now
- Exchange OWA vulnerability CVE-2026-42897 was disclosed May 14: on-premises Exchange admins should confirm patch status immediately; Exchange Online tenants are already covered
- Platform SSO during automated device enrollment is now generally available for macOS: hybrid identity shops running zero-touch provisioning can drop the remaining manual identity steps from their Mac enrollment flow

WORTH YOUR ATTENTION 🔭

- Windows 365 for Agents is in public preview, letting organisations run AI agents on dedicated Cloud PCs with full Intune policy coverage — the managed infrastructure story for agentic AI is taking shape faster than most governance frameworks are ready for
- The Sentinel-to-Defender XDR migration deadline of March 31, 2027 is closer than it sounds; Microsoft is framing this as a security architecture decision, not a portal preference, and the design choices made now will be hard to undo
- Entra Tenant Governance APIs have reached general availability, giving multi-tenant operators and MSPs a programmatic path to managing shadow tenants and enforcing configuration standards at scale


ONE FOR THE HELP DESK 🎫

Teams live events going dark on June 30 will generate questions the moment scheduled events stop appearing or links break. Front-line support teams should have a redirect answer ready pointing users to Town Halls, and comms teams should be notified before they find out the hard way.
